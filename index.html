Nigerian Party Jollof Rice Recipe for 10 People

Ingredients

  • 5 cups (1.2 kg) long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1.5 liters (6 cups) tomato stew base (blend of fresh tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and onions)
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 1 cup (240 ml) vegetable oil
  • 4 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 liters (8 cups) chicken or beef stock
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 2 tablespoons dried thyme
  • 4 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • 2 teaspoons white pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup (150 g) mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, green beans) - optional
  • 500 g cooked chicken, beef, or shrimp (optional for protein)

Equipment

  • Large pot with a tight-fitting lid
  • Wooden spoon
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Blender (for tomato stew base)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tomato Stew Base: Blend 10 large fresh tomatoes, 4 red bell peppers, 2 scotch bonnet peppers, and 1 onion until smooth. Boil the mixture in a pot over medium heat for 20-30 minutes until it thickens and reduces. Set aside.
  2. Wash the Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly in cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch. Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry the Base: Heat 1 cup of vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add sliced onions and sauté until translucent (about 5 minutes). Stir in 4 tablespoons of tomato paste and fry for 3-5 minutes until it darkens slightly.
  4. Add Tomato Stew: Pour in the prepared tomato stew base and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  5. Season the Base: Add curry powder, thyme, seasoning cubes, white pepper, and bay leaves. Stir well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Add Stock: Pour in 2 liters of chicken or beef stock. Taste and adjust with salt. Bring to a boil.
  7. Cook the Rice: Add the washed rice to the pot, stirring to ensure it’s fully coated with the stew. Reduce heat to low, cover with a tight-fitting lid, and cook for 30-40 minutes. Check occasionally, adding a little water (1/4 cup at a time) if needed to prevent burning.
  8. Add Vegetables and Protein: When the rice is almost done (about 80% cooked), stir in the mixed vegetables and cooked protein (if using). Cover and cook for an additional 5-10 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and has absorbed the flavors.
  9. Check Doneness: The rice should be soft but not mushy, with a vibrant red color. Remove bay leaves and fluff with a fork.
  10. Serve: Serve hot with sides like fried plantain, coleslaw, or grilled chicken.

Tips for Authentic Party Jollof

  • Use parboiled rice for the best texture, as it holds up well during cooking.
  • For a smoky flavor, let the rice slightly burn at the bottom (optional, but traditional for party jollof).
  • Adjust scotch bonnet peppers to control spiciness.
  • Prepare the tomato stew base in advance to save time.

Note: Cooking times may vary depending on heat source and pot size. Always taste and adjust seasoning to preference.
